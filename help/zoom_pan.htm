Zoom and Pan

Zooming with the Mouse Wheel

Roll the mouse wheel over the image to zoom in or out:

  • Scroll up — zoom in (+15 % per notch)
  • Scroll down — zoom out (−15 % per notch)

The zoom is anchored to the cursor position: the pixel under the mouse pointer stays in place as you zoom, making it easy to focus on a specific area.

Zoom range: 5 % to 2000 %.

Tip: To return to the fit-to-window view, click the image file in the tree again — this reloads it at the default fit zoom.

Panning (Scrolling)

When the image is larger than the window you can pan in two ways:

  • Click and drag — hold the left mouse button and drag to move around the image. The cursor changes to a hand ✍ to indicate pan mode, and to a four-arrow cursor ✥ while dragging.
  • Scroll bars — horizontal and vertical scroll bars appear automatically when the image exceeds the window size. They disappear when the image fits entirely in the window.

Fit to Window

Every time you select a new image it is automatically scaled to fit the window (maximum 100 % — the image is never enlarged above its actual pixel size on load).
